Steak Frites Recipe

Description

A timeless French bistro classic, this steak frites recipe features a perfectly seared steak paired with a generous pile of crispy, golden fries. A simple yet elegant meal, it comes together quickly for a delicious weeknight dinner or a special at-home date night. The dish is often served with a rich, savory sauce, such as a compound herb butter or a creamy peppercorn sauce, drizzled over the juicy, tender steak.

Ingredients

  • 3 russet potatoes, cut into strips
  • 2 12-ounce New York strip steaks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 dozen sprigs thyme, fresh
  • 1 dozen cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, room temperature
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

Fries/Frites

  1. Soak the fries in a bowl of ice water and agitate to remove extra starch.
  2. Rinse the fries and dry thoroughly. If there is time, freeze the potatoes and allow them to thaw before cooking.
  3. Place fries into deep fryer at 300 degrees for 3 minutes. Remove from oil and place on a paper towel to soak up any excess oil.
  4. Set aside and preheat the oven for 350F. These will be cooked for 5 minutes immediately prior to serving the completed dish.

Steak

  1. Generously season all sides of the steak with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over high heat and add the steak once smoking, reducing the heat to medium.
  3. Allow the steak to sear, about 3 minutes, before flipping.
  4. Add thyme, garlic and butter to the pan, and cook for another 3 minutes (for medium-rare) while basting with a spoon.
  5. Remove steak from the pan and allow to rest for 10 minutes before serving.
  6. After the steak has rested and the fries are out of the oven, plate together with your favorite sauce.

Enjoy!
