Apple Pie Recipe

Description

Apple pie is a beloved American dessert featuring a flaky, buttery crust filled with tender, cinnamon-spiced apples. This classic comfort food balances the sweet and tart flavors of the apples with a warm, cozy aroma that is irresistible. Perfect for any occasion, it can be served warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or enjoyed on its own.

Ingredients

  • 8 Granny Smith Apples
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 tablespoon cinnamon
  • 9 inch double-crust pie pastry, room temperature

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 425F (220C).
  2. Peel, core, and cut the apples into thin slices. Sprinkle with cinnamon and set aside for later.
  3. Brown butter in a saucepan, then stir in the flour until pasty and fragrant, about 1-2 minutes.
  4. Add sugars and water, then bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce to a simmer for 4 minutes.
  5. Place one of pie pastries into a greased pie dish, then cut the other pastry into 1-inch strips.
  6. Fill the pastry in the pie dish with the cinnamon-apple mixture, slightly mounded.
  7. Weave the pastry strips over top of the apples and pinch the strip ends into the bottom pastry edges.
  8. Drizzle the sugar-butter glaze over the pastry lattice and into the apples beneath, but take care not to drip down the sides into the dish.
  9. Bake for 15 minutes at 425F (220C), then reduce to 350F (175 C) for another 40 minutes, until apples are soft
  10. Serve topped with ice cream!

Enjoy!
